About Me

Hello! My name is Dexter White and I am a senior computer science major currently attending Texas A&M University. I am also pursuing a minor in cybersecurity as well! It feels like I have been interested in computer science for my whole life, but this field really became more than a hobby in my junior year of highschool when I took my first computer science class. Since then, I have gotten so much more exciting and valuable experience during my time as both a computer science major at Texas A&M as well as a software engineering intern at Visa!

A bit more about me is that I don't keep my passion for computer science limited to solely what I learn in the classroom. I am always trying to find ways to learn and try new concepts and technologies, as well as share my knowledge with others! One way I have achieved this is through my position as a teaching assistant under Dr. Martin Carlisle, a professor of practice at Texas A&M. Working as a teaching assistant not only allows me to reinforce my knowledge of past computer science topics I have learned, but it also allows me to share my love of the field with other, newer students! Additionally, I really enjoy working on personal projects as well as attending hackathons in my spare time. A little fun fact is that my team and I actually won second place at the 2021 TAMUhack hackathon!
