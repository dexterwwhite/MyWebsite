Fields of Interest

Currently, my main fields of interest in the world of computer science are software engineering and web development. During my time as a software engineering intern at Visa this past summer, I really enjoyed working on my full-stack project. It was very fulfilling to create a RESTful API backend and then showcase data from this API on the frontend of our site. I plan on studying and gaining more experience within the field of full-stack development in the future, as it has really piqued my interest! Additionally, software engineering as a whole really intrigues me, and I will continue to work hard at becoming one post-graduation!

Aggie Miracle

Aggie Miracle is a philanthropic organization at Texas A&M that has a goal of raising funds and awareness for the McLane Children's Medical Center in Temple, Texas. Aggie Miracle has been a passion of mine for the past 2 years, and for my upcoming senior year I will be continuing in the organization for my third year! For the past 2 years I was a committee member in the Miracle Maker Relation's committee (which has a focus on recruiting other students to come and participate at our big event, AggieTHON) and in the logistics committee (focuses on providing food for AggieTHON, as well as helping other committees plan/setup their work). For this upcoming year, I plan on applying for a director role, meaning I will lead my own committee for the year. After being in the organization for so long, it is only fitting that I would move into a leadership role in order to lead and share my passion with a group of newer members.

Aggie Web Developers

My interest in web development definitely began to really manifest during my junior year. During this time, I was working on web development projects in several of my classes as well as spending time participating the Aggie Web Developers club. The focus of this club was to share web development knowledge and experience with its members, and each weekly meeting would involve a new tool/framework and us practicing using it. Some examples of meeting topics were ReactJS, SQL Databases, and Postman. Additionally, an example of one of the projects I made within this club was a URL-shortener website. Overall, it was very fun to take part in this club and it really helped drive my passion for web development even further!
