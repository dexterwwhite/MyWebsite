Fields of Interest

Currently, my main fields of interest in the world of computer science are software engineering and web development. During my time as a software engineering intern at Visa this past summer, it was very fulfilling to create a RESTful API backend and then showcase data from this API on the frontend of our site. I plan on studying and gaining more experience within the field of full-stack development in the future, as it has really piqued my interest! When it comes to software engineering, I am equally as thrilled by the prospects of this field. The process of being able to build up a solution to a problem from scratch is very compelling, and it almost feels as if you are working through a puzzle while coding. While these two fields are the ones that stand out the most to me at the time, there is still so much more within the realm of computer science that I would love to experience. Regardless of where I end up post-graduation, though, I know I will give it my very best!

Aggie Miracle

Aggie Miracle is a philanthropic organization at Texas A&M that has a goal of raising funds and awareness for the McLane Children's Medical Center in Temple, Texas. Aggie Miracle has been a passion of mine for the past 2 years, and for my upcoming senior year I will be continuing in the organization for my third year! Previously, I was a committee member in the Miracle Maker Relation's committee (focuses on recruiting other students to participate at our big event, AggieTHON) and in the logistics committee (focuses on providing food for AggieTHON, as well as helping other committees plan/setup their work). For this upcoming year, I have been selected to serve as the Director of Special Events! For this role, I will be leading a group of committee members to boost awareness and support for our cause through setting up special events. These events will include holidays and special days for the medical center, as well as any other occasions my committee thinks up. For my third and final year, I will give my all to be the best director I can be!

Aggie Web Developers

My interest in web development definitely began to really manifest during my junior year. During this time, I was working on web development projects in several of my classes as well as spending time participating the Aggie Web Developers club. The focus of this club was to share web development knowledge and experience with its members, and each weekly meeting would involve a new tool/framework and us practicing using it. Some examples of meeting topics were ReactJS, SQL Databases, and Postman. Additionally, an example of one of the projects I made within this club was a URL-shortener website. Overall, it was very fun to take part in this club and it really helped drive my passion for web development even further!
