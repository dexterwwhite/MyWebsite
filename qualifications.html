Experience

Visa Software Engineering Intern -- Summer 2022

It is hard to put into words how much I have learned and how much I have grown as a software engineer throughout my time at Visa. To begin with, my main project for the summer involved creating a Dependency Mapping Service for over 130 internal applications as well as utilizing this service within the UI of an internal website. I approached this challenge by creating a service that consisted of a new database table, a Spring Boot service, and a Spring Boot RESTful API endpoint. My service replaced functions that would take over a minute to compute with functions that would take under a second to compute. On our internal website (built with ReactJS), I utilized Redux in order to display the dependency mapping and accountability information. This project had been an existing problem for the team I worked with, so my work was directly useful to the team and the company as a whole! Throughout my time there, my team used the Agile software development methodology which meant I was able to get first-hand experience with using Agile at an enterprise level. Additionally, I was taught and utilized enterprise-level git practices, which was very beneficial when it came to working within my team. One last point to bring up is that I worked with 3 other interns throughout the summer which taught me a lot about planning, sharing of work, and team collaboration. Overall, I learned so much and was able to build up an invaluable amount of experience as a software engineer, and I know I will use what this internship has taught me throughout the rest of my career.

Teaching Assistant -- Fall 2021 to present

Since the fall of my Junior year, I have been a TA under Dr. Martin Carlisle. In that very first semester, I held this position for the class Introduction to Program Design and Concepts. The responsibilities for this class involved running a lab section, holding office hours, and grading. Each week during lab I would lead a class of 30 students through a programming assignment, all the while helping out and answering any students' questions. I would also hold several hours of office hours each week that were typically very busy (back-to-back students for the entire duration!). These office hours would consist of helping students identify bugs in their weekly programming assignment as well as teaching them debugging strategies and other coding best practices. For the spring of 2022 and this upcoming fall of 2022, I was and will continue to be a teaching assistant for the class Discrete Structures. This position involves a slight shift in responsibilities from the previous class to mainly office hours and grading. The office hours for this class typically consist of me sharing my knowledge of discrete math topics and working students through problems related to these topics. Overall, being a teaching assistant has helped me grow an incredible amount as a computer science student. Not only has it helped reinforce my knowledge of the introductory computer science topics, it has also taught me how to share this knowledge with others and instill problem solving strategies within my students.
